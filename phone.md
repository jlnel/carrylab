      <h3>Description</h3>
      <p>My phone is my connection to the digital world. It's in a pink case with a Toro Inoue sticker.</p>
      
      <p>This device has replaced so many other items I don’t have to carry: camera, music, notes, watch, GPS, flashlight, and even my wallet in some situations. I love how much functionality is packed into this pocket sized computer.</p>
    </div>

    <div class="why-essential">
      <h3>Why I Can't Leave Home Without It</h3>
      <p>My phone is how people reach me, whether it's a call from family or a text from a friend. Being unreachable creates anxiety both for me and for the people who might need to contact me.</p>
      
      <p>But it's more than just communication. My phone is my navigation system when I'm driving to unfamiliar places. It's my payment method if I want to use Apple Pay. It's my entertainment during boring waits and long commutes.</p>
      
      <p>The few times I've accidentally left home without my phone, I've felt a sense of panic and disconnection. I instinctively reach for it dozens of times throughout the day. Whether I'm checking the time, taking a photo, looking up information, or just killing time scrolling through social media, my phone is always there.</p>
      
      <p>While some might argue we're too dependent on our phones, I see mine as a tool that enhances my life. It keeps me organized, connected, informed, and entertained. In an emergency, it could be a lifeline. That's why it's absolutely essential to my everyday carry.</p>
